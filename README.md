# 🛒 Global Superstore Sales Analysis — Power BI Dashboard

> An interactive business intelligence dashboard analysing sales, profitability, and returns across global markets using the Global Superstore dataset.

---

## 📊 Project Overview

This project analyses the Global Superstore dataset to uncover key business insights across product categories, markets, countries, and customer segments. The dashboard is built across 4 pages — Sales Overview, Profit Analysis, Returns Analysis, and an Executive Summary with actionable business recommendations.

## 📂 Power BI File
The Power BI dashboard file ([E-Commerce Project.pbix](./E-Commerce%20Project.pbix)) is included in this repository and can be opened using Microsoft Power BI Desktop.
To explore the dashboard interactively, download the PBIX file and open it using Power BI Desktop.

---

## 🎯 Objective

- Identify top-performing markets and product categories by sales and profit
- Analyse profit margins across categories and regions
- Understand return patterns by market, segment, and order priority
- Derive actionable business insights to support strategic decision-making

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 💰 Revenue Performance
- Technology is the highest revenue-generating category, followed by Furniture and Office Supplies
- APAC contributes the largest share of overall sales (30.41%), with EU and US also being significant markets
- United States is the leading country by sales volume among all countries analysed

### 📈 Profitability Analysis
- Technology is the most profitable product category, indicating strong revenue generation with healthy margins
- APAC is the highest-profit market (0.44M), while Africa, EMEA, and Canada contribute comparatively lower profits
- United States is the top contributor to overall profit, followed by Australia and France
- Overall Profit Margin stands at 11.61%

### 🔄 Return Analysis
- The overall order return rate is 4.68%
- LATAM records the highest number of returned orders among all markets
- Consumer Segment accounts for the largest share of returned orders (599 returns)
- Medium and High priority orders account for a substantial share of total returned orders — further rate-based analysis is recommended before drawing conclusions on priority-level return likelihood
- Returns increased year on year from 209 in 2011 to 388 in 2014
